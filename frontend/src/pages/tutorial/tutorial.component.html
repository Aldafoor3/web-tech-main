Binary to Decimal Conversion Tutorial

In this tutorial, you will learn how to convert a binary number (base 2) into its decimal equivalent (base 10). Follow these simple steps to understand the conversion process.

Steps to Convert Binary to Decimal

  1. Write down the binary number.
  2. Starting from the rightmost digit (the least significant bit), assign powers of 2 to each digit. The rightmost digit is multiplied by 2^0, the next one by 2^1, then 2^2, and so on.
  3. Multiply each binary digit by the corresponding power of 2.
  4. Sum the results of these multiplications to get the decimal value.

Example 1: Convert 1011 (Binary) to Decimal

Step-by-Step Process:

Binary number: 1011

Assign powers of 2 from right to left:

1 * 2^3 = 1 * 8 = 8
0 * 2^2 = 0 * 4 = 0
1 * 2^1 = 1 * 2 = 2
1 * 2^0 = 1 * 1 = 1

Sum the results:

8 + 0 + 2 + 1 = 11

The decimal equivalent of 1011 (binary) is 11.

Example 2: Convert 11010 (Binary) to Decimal

Step-by-Step Process:

Binary number: 11010

Assign powers of 2 from right to left:

1 * 2^4 = 1 * 16 = 16
1 * 2^3 = 1 * 8 = 8
0 * 2^2 = 0 * 4 = 0
1 * 2^1 = 1 * 2 = 2
0 * 2^0 = 0 * 1 = 0

Sum the results:

16 + 8 + 0 + 2 + 0 = 26

The decimal equivalent of 11010 (binary) is 26.

Summary

To convert a binary number to decimal, you need to multiply each binary digit by its corresponding power of 2 and sum the results. This simple method allows you to easily convert any binary number to its decimal form.

Decimal to Binary Conversion Tutorial

In this tutorial, you will learn how to convert a decimal number (base 10) into its binary equivalent (base 2). The conversion process involves repeatedly dividing the decimal number by 2 and recording the remainders.

Steps to Convert Decimal to Binary

  1. Write down the decimal number you want to convert.
  2. Divide the decimal number by 2.
  3. Record the remainder of the division (either 0 or 1).
  4. Divide the quotient (the result from the division) by 2 again, and record the new remainder.
  5. Repeat the process until the quotient becomes 0.
  6. Write the remainders in reverse order (starting from the last remainder). This is the binary equivalent of the decimal number.

Example 1: Convert 13 (Decimal) to Binary

Step-by-Step Process:

Decimal number: 13

Divide by 2:

13 ÷ 2 = 6 remainder 1

Divide the quotient (6) by 2:

6 ÷ 2 = 3 remainder 0

Divide the quotient (3) by 2:

3 ÷ 2 = 1 remainder 1

Divide the quotient (1) by 2:

1 ÷ 2 = 0 remainder 1

Write the remainders in reverse order:

1 1 0 1

The binary equivalent of 13 (decimal) is 1101.

Example 2: Convert 25 (Decimal) to Binary

Step-by-Step Process:

Decimal number: 25

Divide by 2:

25 ÷ 2 = 12 remainder 1

Divide the quotient (12) by 2:

12 ÷ 2 = 6 remainder 0

Divide the quotient (6) by 2:

6 ÷ 2 = 3 remainder 0

Divide the quotient (3) by 2:

3 ÷ 2 = 1 remainder 1

Divide the quotient (1) by 2:

1 ÷ 2 = 0 remainder 1

Write the remainders in reverse order:

1 1 0 0 1

The binary equivalent of 25 (decimal) is 11001.

Summary

To convert a decimal number to binary, you repeatedly divide the number by 2, keeping track of the remainders. Once the quotient reaches 0, writing the remainders in reverse order gives you the binary equivalent of the decimal number.

Decimal to Hexadecimal Conversion Tutorial

In this tutorial, you will learn how to convert a decimal number (base 10) into its hexadecimal equivalent (base 16). The conversion process involves repeatedly dividing the decimal number by 16 and recording the remainders.

Steps to Convert Decimal to Hexadecimal

  1. Write down the decimal number you want to convert.
  2. Divide the decimal number by 16.
  3. Record the remainder of the division. If the remainder is 10 or higher, replace it with the corresponding hexadecimal letter (A = 10, B = 11, ..., F = 15).
  4. Divide the quotient (the result of the division) by 16 again, and record the new remainder.
  5. Repeat the process until the quotient becomes 0.
  6. Write the remainders in reverse order (starting from the last remainder). This is the hexadecimal equivalent of the decimal number.

Example 1: Convert 254 (Decimal) to Hexadecimal

Step-by-Step Process:

Decimal number: 254

Divide by 16:

254 ÷ 16 = 15 remainder 14 (14 = E in hexadecimal)

Divide the quotient (15) by 16:

15 ÷ 16 = 0 remainder 15 (15 = F in hexadecimal)

Write the remainders in reverse order:

F E

The hexadecimal equivalent of 254 (decimal) is FE.

Example 2: Convert 678 (Decimal) to Hexadecimal

Step-by-Step Process:

Decimal number: 678

Divide by 16:

678 ÷ 16 = 42 remainder 6

Divide the quotient (42) by 16:

42 ÷ 16 = 2 remainder 10 (10 = A in hexadecimal)

Divide the quotient (2) by 16:

2 ÷ 16 = 0 remainder 2

Write the remainders in reverse order:

2 A 6

The hexadecimal equivalent of 678 (decimal) is 2A6.

Summary

To convert a decimal number to hexadecimal, you repeatedly divide the number by 16, keeping track of the remainders. Once the quotient reaches 0, writing the remainders in reverse order gives you the hexadecimal equivalent of the decimal number.

Hexadecimal to Decimal Conversion Tutorial

In this tutorial, you will learn how to convert a hexadecimal number (base 16) into its decimal equivalent (base 10). The conversion process involves expanding the hexadecimal number and multiplying each digit by the power of 16.

Steps to Convert Hexadecimal to Decimal

  1. Write down the hexadecimal number you want to convert.
  2. Starting from the rightmost digit, assign a position to each digit, starting from 0. Each position will represent a power of 16 (160, 161, 162, ...).
  3. Convert each hexadecimal digit to its decimal equivalent (A = 10, B = 11, C = 12, D = 13, E = 14, F = 15).
  4. Multiply each digit by 16 raised to the power of its position.
  5. Sum all the results to get the decimal equivalent.

Example 1: Convert 1F (Hexadecimal) to Decimal

Step-by-Step Process:

Hexadecimal number: 1F

Assign positions starting from the right:

1 (position 1), F (position 0)

Convert hexadecimal digits to decimal:

1 → 1
F → 15

Now, multiply each digit by 16 raised to the power of its position: 

1 × 161 = 16
15 × 160 = 15

Sum the results:

16 + 15 = 31

The decimal equivalent of 1F (hexadecimal) is 31.

Example 2: Convert A3 (Hexadecimal) to Decimal

Step-by-Step Process:

Hexadecimal number: A3

Assign positions starting from the right:

A (position 1), 3 (position 0)

Convert hexadecimal digits to decimal:

A → 10
3 → 3

Now, multiply each digit by 16 raised to the power of its position: 

10 × 161 = 160
3 × 160 = 3

Sum the results:

160 + 3 = 163

The decimal equivalent of A3 (hexadecimal) is 163.

Summary

To convert a hexadecimal number to decimal, first assign positions to each digit, starting from the right. Convert each hexadecimal digit to its decimal equivalent, multiply it by 16 raised to the power of its position, and sum the results to get the decimal equivalent.
